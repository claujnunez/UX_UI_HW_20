Startup Responsive Website "OptiMeal"

We created Optimeal RWD to Novigi startup company. We delivered an inclusive, easy to navigate, aesthetic, and engaging design. User insights were a key while creating an intuitive concept through prototyping.

Project Overview

Duration: 3 Weeks
Date: Oct/2020
My Role: UXUI Designer
Tools: Figma, Adobe XD, Adobe Color Picker, Fireshot, Google

What OptiMeal does

Optimeal allows the user to create recepies according to their culinary taste, swap ingredients from their pantry, and create a meal plan within their budget.

Challenges

Convincing the client that RWW is a great starting point instead of an app due to their budget, and that the target market was Millenials not Gen X.

Accomplishments

We supported with data the appropiate nitche and created an intuitive website design, with an easy navigation, and with a quick sign up.

The Story


We met an amazing group of entreprenurs who shared their idea of a new app called OptiMeal and their mission to support the economy on every household by helping people to stick to their budget, and buy ingredients to cook at home and stay safe during COVID-19 Pandemic. Thier mockups and marketing research open up our eyes on how we might help them to create a great design an a differnet verison of their idea focusing on the user's needs, balancing their marketing strategy, and guide them in the right direction on their investmet.

Stakeholder Brief

NOVIGI's interest is to support economy on every houshold during COVID-19 Pandemic by creating a product that simplifies shopping ingrediets online using any device, keeping people safe by providing meals to cook from their favorite cuisine, and stick to thier budget. Their proto persona was based on a broad niche in the market from Generation Z to Generation X. Their mockups and sketches showed us the facinated idea for creating an app, and their research data, also provided their initial investment. The Problem


OptiMeal is seeking to identify the pain points that people encounter in their grocery shopping online, their motivations, specific needs, and what keep them from buying.
We have observed that users aim to follow a budget, but failed to create a grocery list according to what they already have in their pantry and are limited to find grocery options in person at the supermarket or ordering online.

The Solution


Our solution is to design a innovative, useful, efficient, intuitive and memorable user centered responsive website where the user can connect via mobile and desktop with a logical navigation where they can plan, prepare meals within their budget, provide recipes according to their culinary taste, swap ingredients from what they have on their pantry, generate shopping lists, and provide list of supermarkets with different price offers.

OptiMeal Competitor's Analysis





Iterview Plan

We planned and conducted ten interviews to discover the pain points that people encounter when grocery shopping.
We surveyed 73 participants,primarily between the ages of 21-40, who are considered frequent grocery shoppers that like to cook.

Interview Objectives:

  • Understand how people search for and pick recipes.
  • Understand how we to provide the best deals with users' local stores and delivery services.
  • Understand the needs of users when they are grocery shopping for their meals.

Methods:

We conducted 1-1 Qualitive Interviews = 10 users
We conducted 1-1 Quantitative Survey = 73 users
Surveys were released in social media = FB and Twitter
